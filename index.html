POLLUTION PROBE

Non-Profit Website Redesign

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Pollution Probe is Canada’s oldest NGO that is working actively to conserve Canadian environment fore more than 50 years now. However, their website design looks outdated without a clear mission statement and unpolished user experience. An overhaul is overdue and will ensure Pollution Probe showcase their rich history of valuable contribution digitally that will encourage people to assist the organization’s initiatives

We developed a Design Language System that expanded on the foundation laid by the existing brand identity ensuring functionality and consistency across the website to elevate the user experience

DESIGN METHODOLOGY & DELIVERABLES

In this redesign project, we set out to create a brand-new design system for the Pollution Probe website. We ran 2 weeks of design sprints based on the design thinking methodology, which includes 5 stages: empathize, define, ideate, prototype and test. We eventually redesigned their logo and 3 key webpages:
1. Home Page
2. About Us Page
3. Donation Page

DISCOVERY PHASE

Goal : To understand the current industry standards for environment related NGO websites and our users expectations through research.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

We conducted a heuristic evaluation of the current website to become familiar with its current structure and identify the usability issues related to navigation and visual elements.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Lack of visual accents to identify CTAs
2. User control and freedom not supported
3. Out of date aesthetic and looks unprofessional
4. Ambiguous navigation titles
5. Unnecessary animation

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

We also conducted a competitor analysis of the features and design styles to understand the market that Pollution Probe is in, to have an idea of what's available to customers now, and how we can do things better.

USER INTERVIEWS

I interviewed 5 potential users to understand their expectations from an NGO website and know about issues they might have faced in the past. We defined 3 research objectives and planned 10 open-ended questions to gain deep insights.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Users want to know about NGO’s history before trusting them with money
2. The website should look credible
3. The donation form should ask only relevant questions

STAKEHOLDER INTERVIEW

We interviewed the Research & Communications Coordinator of Pollution Probe to understand the agency’s vision for the redesign.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Showcase organization’s rich history effectively
2. Preferably have an interactive timeline
3. Have more visuals, smooth and meaningful transitions

SURVEY & SOCIAL MEDIA POLLS

For the quantitative research, we did a google survey of 10 questions which received 40 responses. The results helped us interpret the age of potential users, their source of information and their level of concern for the environment.

We also utilized platforms like Instagram to gain insights on the same questions.

DEFINITION PHASE

Goal : To understand the wants and needs of users by analyzing the research data

AFFINITY DIAGRAM

We analyzed the research data by grouping it based on the similarities to identify trends in users’ expectations and motivations.

USER PERSONA

After consolidating and analysing the research data gathered, we came up with 1 user persona that best describe the demographics of Pollution Probe’s audience. This allowed us and to constantly be reminded of the people that we are designing the product for, their needs and wants, as well as pain points.

I LIKE, I WISH, WHAT IF

We used the I Like, I Wish and What If tool to pitch ideas, rank them and eventually – select the best of them for inclusion into the project.

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION MATRIX

Our top ideas on Feature Prioritization Matrix include:
1. Easy to use, interactive website
2. Showcase the “who and what’ about the organization front and centre
3. Bring more focus on donation tab on the homepage

IDEATION PHASE

Goal : To devise ideas/solutions to cater the concerns and challenges identified earlier in the process.

CARD SORTING

We moved on to evaluate the current navigation of the existing website. We changed 2 titles and removed 5 duplicate/redundant nav items to streamline navigation.

SITE MAP

My team member, Emu, developed site map based on the card sorting and enhanced the global utilities based on current standards and our user research.

STORY BOARD

Leveraging on our research on customers’ needs, wants and expectations, I prepared a storyboard that will be used to guide the lifecycle of product development.

USER FLOW DIAGRAM

Based on our storyboard, we identified two tasks that would help Dianna achieve her goal of:
1. making a contribution
2. get more people aligned with environment conservation

DESIGN PHASE

Goal : To put our ideas into action and evaluate them against users expectations

LO-FI WIREFRAMES

Our homepage wireframe was a mix of our individual versions but the other two pages were designed individually and I am responsible for the Donation Page design.

The concept of our wireframes is based on the industry design trends and competitor analysis and aims to be:
1. Functional and conservative
2. Sophisticated and modern

FIRST USER TEST

To identify usability issues early in the process, we conducted one set of user tests on our low-fidelity wireframes prepared in Figma.
We performed 4 user tests on lo-fi prototype with the following objective & tasks:
Objective:
Determine if the redesigned website works as intended or not Tasks:
1. Find further information about the organization
2. Make a donation
3. Share the homepage with a friend

TEST RESULT ANALYSIS

After carefully noting and noticing the said and unsaid feedback from users, we analyzed and grouped the user test notes and.colour coded them based on the frequency of response. Most issues were related to the donation page flow and thus, I needed to redesign it.

ITERATIONS

Based on the user testing feedback, I changed the content order to streamline the donation process. Simultaneously, we came up with a basic design language to guide a style for our final mock ups.

PROTOTYPE & TEST PHASE

Goal : To polish our suggested solution through extensive testing

HI-FI WIREFRAMES

The inspiration for the colours of our is largely taken from the colours of the existing website to give it a fresh look and yet keep it related with the original site. The redesign of donation page was completed followed by the application of our commonly decided UI Elements to yield high fidelity mockups.

SECOND USER TEST RESULT

We carried out another round of user tests on the same tasks as round 1 using our hi-fi prototype to hear user’s feedback on the usability, navigation and features particularly of the donation page.

While the overall confusion of the donation page had improved, we found two new issues :


❌ Some users did not notice the contents underneath of the donation form.

❌ The timeline on the about page was not intuitive.

ITERATIONS - 2

Added a link to see other donation options on the prompt, which users will see after making a donation, so we can encourage users to go to see other sections of the page.

ITERATIONS-2

Added an indicator for the currently visible year. Now users are easily able to see where they are at in the whole timeline.

TAKEAWAYS

This project gave us the opportunity to work with an actual client. It was both challenging and interesting to map the expectations of both the client and the users to design an optimal solution for a good user experience. I wish, we had heard back from Sabah (our stakeholder) to have feedback on our final product.


The major traffic of the website is of the users looking to download research reports, a section that I intend to work on in future. Another thing that I would like to improve is the aesthetics of the interactive timeline using subtle micro-interactions and interesting graphics.


Seeing the initial donation page layout fail was a real eye-opener. Even though it was based on the key user research takeaways, yet the design was doomed to fail as it did not match the existing mental model of the users.
Nevertheless, it was an important moment that reminded me the importance of testing early in order to fail, evaluate and reiterate to create truly usable, polished products and experiences.
